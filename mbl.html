Jamming of Metal Sheets using EPMs for Stiffness Modulation

View Publication

Jamming Techniques Haptics Circuit Design

Overview

As part of the Morphable Biorobotics Lab at Boston University, I designed a flexible PCB to demonstrate the application of magnetically jammed metal sheets for haptic feedback in wearable devices. The system triggered four electropermanent magnets to control the jamming mechanism, enabling variable stiffness for enhanced tactile experiences. The flexible PCB allowed users to wear it comfortably on their hands, eliminating the need for bulky external control hardware. The PCB was fabricated by JLCPCB.

Schematic Diagram

  • The system is powered by a 12 V supply, which drives the BTN8962 driver. A voltage regulator steps the 12 V down to 9 V to supply the Arduino Nano. The Nano outputs control signals to the BTN via pull-up resistors.
  • When triggered, the BTN drives its corresponding electropermanent magnet with a high-current pulse, causing the magnet to jam the attached metal sheets.
