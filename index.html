I’m Sru, a research associate in Soft Robots Lab at Wesleyan University, supervised by Professor Sonia Roberts.

At Soft Robots Lab, we're developing bioinspired foot structures with varying toes configurations to jump efficiently on sand.

My work involves developing robotic platform for testing different foot structures to investigate jumping on granular media.

My research interest lies in taking inspiration from nature and developing novel robot designs, sensing, and control strategies for multi-domain exploration and medical technologies.

Origami Inspired Actuator

Advisor: Professor Douglas Holmes
Mechanics of Slender Structures Lab (MOSS), Boston University

I developed Yoshimura origami patterns capable of extension and flexion. Fabricated the actuators via laser-cut patterned sheets and optimized performance by testing different silicone compositions and actuator geometries. Conducted experiments to measure grip strength and bending curvature, analyzing how design and materials affected performance.
