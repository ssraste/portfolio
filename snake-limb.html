Soft Snake Limb

Soft Actuators Finite Element Analysis Mechanical Testing
  • My team and I, designed a soft robotic snake limb composed of three double PneuNet actuators, with a granular jamming gripper serving as the mouth for object manipulation.
  • We also develop a variety of soft actuators and characterize their mechanical performance, gaining hands-on experience with fabrication and testing techniques.

    • Soft snake limb

  • We developed a soft robotic snake limb that can move effectively in unstructured environments due to its flexibility and biomimetic design. We created a trapezoidal pneunet structure to enhance stability and adaptability. One limb has total of of 3 actuators.

    • Iterations

  • The initial circular pneunet structure had stability issues, leading to a redesign into a trapezoidal shape for greater robustness.

  • Air bubbles often appeared when casting Dragon Skin 10 silicone, requiring careful fabrication techniques.

    • Fiber-reinforced Actuator

  • We prepared the actuator by pouring Dragonskin 10 silicone into a pre-made mold to cure in segments. Halfway through, we wrapped the fiber threads around the actuator to form the reinforcement design - and a final curing step was performed to attach a strip of fabric to the bottom of this actuator (strain-limiting layer)

    • McKibben artificial muscle

  • McKibben actuator was fabricated by encasing a thin tubing with a constrictive yet expandable mesh, and securing the tubing with Loctite Flexible Adhesive and zip ties

    • PneuNet Actuator

  • We poured dragonskin 10 into a premade mold at intervals to form the chamber designs within the actuator The final cure consisted of adding a fabric strip to the flat bottom of the Pneu-Net as a strain-limiting layer.

  • We did FEA simulation in Abaqus.

    • Shape Memory Alloy (SMA)

  • We programmed a SMA actuator that contracts upon electrical heating, causing it to bend like a finger joint.

    • Soft Resistive sensor

  • We created s soft silicone-based sensor with an embedded conductive strip. As the resisitve sensor stretches, the output voltage reading decreases.
  • Mechanical Testing Results: We found that the resistive sensor was highly responsive to tension and shear, but only slightly to compression, with maximum sensitivity along the zig-zag axis.

    • Soft Capacitive sensor

  • We created a flexible silicone sensor with conductive layers. As the sensor stretches, the length increases and the output voltage reading increases.
  • Mechanical Testing Results: We found that the capacitive sensor responded well to tension and compression but was insensitive to shear and showed some noise.
