    - Gesture controlled car

  • The robot is controlled by a PCB embedded in a glove, allowing wireless control through hand gestures.
  • The MPU6050 detects hand tilt and sends data to an Arduino Nano via I2C. The Arduino transmits movement commands over Bluetooth (HC-05) to the car via UART, where another Arduino Mega relays signals to an L293D motor driver to control the DC motors attached to car's wheels..

    - Speed Control of Split-Phase Induction Motor Using Thyristor-Based Cycloconverter

    - Approximately soft body dynamics with rigid body methods

  • Traditional rigid body dynamics struggle to model soft body behavior accurately, as large deformations introduce a nonlinear stress-strain relationship. I model a soft PneuNet actuator with a hybrid method blending rigid and soft body dynamics.
  • This approach captures the nonlinear behavior typical of hyperelastic materials while treating the structure as a series of rigid links with cumulative displacements.

    • a. Pressure Force \( (F_{\text{pressure}}) = \frac{P \cdot A_0}{1 + P/E} \)

    b. Spring Force \( F_{\text{spring}} = - P \cdot A_0 \)

    c. Damping Force\( F_{\text{damping}} = - c \cdot v \)

    d. Gravity Force\( F_{\text{gravity}} = - m \cdot g \)

    e. Lateral Force \( (F_{\text{lateral}}) = C \cdot \frac{\Delta EI}{L_0^2} \cdot \sin\left(\frac{\pi i}{N}\right) \)

    f. Acceleration \( (a) = \frac{F_{\text{pressure}} + F_{\text{spring}} + F_{\text{damping}} + F_{\text{gravity}} + F_{\text{lateral}}}{m} \)

    g. Pneunet Properties

    Cavity Volume: \(3.9 \times 10^{-8}\) m³, Cavity Mass: 0.0417 kg, Number of Chambers: 7, Young's Modulus: \(220 \times 10^3\) Pa, Strain Limiting Layer: \(w = 16.69 \times 10^{-3}\) m, \(h = 2.95 \times 10^{-3}\) m

  • The simulation is done using 7 joints i.e. 7 chambers and 6 links, the forces acting on these joints and links are inducing some bending effect.
  • The model doesn't account continuous deformation like in Piecewise curvature constant or Continuum Cosserat models.
